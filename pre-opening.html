The 90-Day Framework

What Pre-Opening Leadership Actually Looks Like

Most Miami restaurant openings fail in the first 90 days not because of the food — but because the operational foundation was never built. Here's how I approach it.

D-90

Operational Blueprint

Define org structure, staffing levels, service model, and vendor requirements. Begin SOP drafting for all FOH and BOH departments. Establish the opening budget and labor model.

D-60

Recruiting & Vendor Setup

Begin hiring across all positions. Conduct interviews, reference checks, and onboarding paperwork. Finalize vendor relationships and delivery schedules. Complete training manuals.

D-30

Training & Systems Build

Full team training across service standards, POS systems, menu knowledge, and safety protocols. Run mock services. Coordinate health inspections and permits. Final equipment walkthroughs.

D-7

Soft Opening & Refinement

Invite-only soft opening to test service flow, kitchen timing, and team communication. Debrief after each service. Adjust staffing, SOP gaps, and pacing before the public opening.

D-0

Opening Night

Full operational readiness. All positions staffed and trained. Systems tested. Vendors confirmed. Leadership present on the floor from the first cover to last call.

Common Questions

Pre-Opening FAQ

When should I bring in pre-opening support?

Ideally 90–120 days before your target opening date. The earlier, the better — most problems that cause bad openings are created in the first 60 days of planning.

Do you work with first-time restaurant owners?

Yes. First-time owners benefit the most from pre-opening support because they don't yet know what they don't know. That's exactly the gap this service is built to close.

What types of concepts do you work with?

Independent restaurants, hotel F&B outlets, banquet departments, and catering operations in the Miami – South Florida market. Fine dining, casual, fast-casual, and everything in between.

Can you help if we're already past the 90-day mark?

Yes. A compressed timeline changes the scope and intensity of the work, but it's manageable. The first call will clarify what's realistic given your timeline.
