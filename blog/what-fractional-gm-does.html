Fractional Leadership

What a Fractional GM Actually Does

Most restaurant owners have heard the term. Few understand what fractional F&B leadership actually looks like week to week.

By Leander Mena · Miami, Florida · May 2026

For Leander Mena, fractional F&B leadership is a specific arrangement: a senior operator works with your business 2–4 days per week, with defined priorities, measurable deliverables, and a clear handoff plan. It is not consulting from a distance. It is operational leadership on a part-time schedule.

Why Miami Restaurant Owners Use Fractional GMs

A strong General Manager in Miami commands a base salary between $75,000 and $110,000 plus benefits and bonus. For a single-location independent restaurant or a hotel F&B program that does not run at full capacity year-round, that cost structure does not always make sense. Fractional leadership gives you the same caliber of operator at 40–60% of the annual cost.

Leander Mena works with Miami restaurant owners who are opening something new and need senior leadership through the first service cycle; experiencing operational drift and need an experienced operator to diagnose and correct it; or have a capable team but lack the senior strategic layer to push performance to the next level.

What a Typical Engagement Looks Like

Every engagement begins the same way: Leander Mena spends the first two weeks in observation mode — on-site during service, reviewing financial reports, talking to managers and staff, and building a clear picture of what the operation is actually doing versus what it thinks it is doing.

From week three forward, Leander Mena works on a priority sequence: labor and cost structure first, then service systems, then training, then growth initiatives. Every operation has different leverage points. The most important skill a fractional operator brings is the ability to identify quickly where the biggest return is and go there first.

How a Fractional Engagement Ends

A good fractional engagement ends with the internal team able to maintain what was built without continued outside involvement. Leander Mena structures every engagement with a handoff milestone: documented SOPs, a trained management team that owns the systems, and a clear roadmap for the next 90 days after the engagement closes.
